Introduction

We utilized advanced natural language processing techniques, specifically transformers, to develop a cutting-edge model capable of analyzing any given text and generating insightful questions and corresponding answers. This was achieved through leveraging the power of the pre-trained T5 model, which was developed by Google and is widely regarded as one of the most effective models in the field of natural language processing.


> See more about T5

DataSet

We fine-tuned the T5 model using two datasets: the Stanford Question Answering Dataset (SQuAD) and a custom dataset created from exams at the Sup'Com. The goal of this fine-tuning was to develop a question/answer model that could generate questions and answers from a given text, with a focus on fields related to our own field of expertise.

The SQuAD dataset is a popular benchmark for question answering models and contains a large number of questions and answers on various topics. By fine-tuning the T5 model on this dataset, we aimed to improve the model's ability to understand natural language and generate accurate answers.


> See more about SQuAD

In addition to the SQuAD dataset, we also created a custom dataset using exams from the Sup'Com, which is a higher education institution specializing in information and communication technology. This dataset allowed us to focus on fields related to our own area of expertise, which could improve the model's ability to generate relevant and accurate answers in our specific domain.


Fine-tuning T5: Adaptive fine-tuning

Even though pre-trained language models are more robust in terms of out-of-distribution generalisation than previous models (Hendrycks et al., 2020), they are still poorly equipped to deal with data that is substantially different from the one they have been pre-trained on. Adaptive fine-tuning is a way to bridge such a shift in distribution by fine-tuning the model on data that is closer to the distribution of the target data.
